“Thanks to Afghan Innovate, I learned coding and built an app to help farmers in my village.”— Ahmad,Thanks to Afghan Innovate, I learned coding and built an app to help farmers in my village,” Ahmad shared with a determined smile. Coming from a small rural area in northern Afghanistan, Ahmad faced many challenges, especially since internet connectivity is poor and unreliable. Despite these obstacles, he was motivated to find solutions that could improve local farmers’ lives. Through Afghan Innovate’s training programs, he learned basic coding and how to develop simple mobile tools. With limited internet access, Ahmad designed an offline-friendly app that farmers can use without constant internet connection. The app provides weather updates based on local weather stations, tips for crop management, and a way to connect with nearby buyers when they visit the village. Since launching the app, many farmers have started adopting new techniques and finding better markets, and Ahmad feels proud that even with limited connectivity, he’s helping his community adapt and thrive.

“The Afghan Innovative gave me the opportunity to turn my idea into a real project that helps my community," Laila explained. Coming from a remote village in Afghanistan where internet connectivity is often unreliable, she faced many challenges in developing her project. Despite these obstacles, the Hub provided her with training, mentorship, and access to resources that allowed her to adapt her idea to local conditions. Using offline tools and low-bandwidth solutions, Laila created a simple system to improve access to clean water and health information for villagers. Thanks to the support from the Innovation Hub, her project is now making a tangible difference, even with limited internet, and her community is starting to see positive change.,
